# Marketplace Milestone 13 — Global-Scale Catalog & Inventory Foundation

## Product vision

Yartong's catalog must feel simple for a buyer in Senapati while being structurally capable of representing a global construction, hardware, industrial, home-improvement and materials universe.

The architecture separates **what a product is** from **who sells it** and **where stock physically exists**.

### Canonical catalog layer

- Hierarchical global categories.
- Brands/manufacturers.
- Canonical products.
- Variants/SKUs with structured attributes, packaging and trade identifiers.

### Marketplace layer

- Supplier-specific listings connected to canonical variants.
- Supplier SKU, price, minimum order, delivery availability and lead time.
- Multiple suppliers may sell the same canonical variant without duplicating the product definition.

### Inventory layer

- Multiple inventory locations per supplier.
- On-hand and reserved quantities tracked independently.
- Append-only stock movements for restocks, reservations, releases, sales, returns, damage and corrections.
- Available quantity is derived from on-hand minus reserved stock.

## Design principles

1. **Catalog is not inventory.** Product identity remains stable even when every supplier is out of stock.
2. **Listings are not products.** Prices and seller terms belong to listings, not the canonical product.
3. **Inventory is location-aware.** A supplier can operate warehouses, yards, stores and depots independently.
4. **Never fabricate global coverage.** The data model can scale globally, while the UI only shows products/listings that actually exist in Yartong's database.
5. **International identifiers are optional.** GTIN/barcode and manufacturer part numbers are supported but not mandatory because many local materials lack standardized identifiers.
6. **Structured attributes remain extensible.** Product and variant characteristics use JSON metadata so new categories do not require a database migration for every technical property.
7. **Audit stock changes.** Inventory adjustments append movement records rather than silently overwriting history.
8. **Commerce-ready boundaries.** Reservations are modeled separately from on-hand stock so future carts/orders can reserve inventory safely.

## UX direction

Public catalog discovery is search-first, information-dense and comparison-oriented. Buyers should quickly understand product, variant, supplier, price, minimum order and current availability.

Supplier operations use two focused workspaces:

- **Catalog:** create/manage sellable listings and product identity.
- **Inventory:** manage physical locations, stock balances and movement history.

No fake products, ratings, prices, sales figures or stock are generated by the platform.
